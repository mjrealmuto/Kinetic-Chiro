Services & Techniques

We offer a multitude of treatment options to help you reach your healthcare goals. Our techniques are minimally invasive and are considered the most effective currently available by research. Depending on your case and which treatments you are a candidate for we will use a combination of these methods to optimize your recovery.

Chiropractic Manipulative Therapy

The "adjustment" is a safe and effective way to introduce normal motion back into a joint. It is useful in the temporary relief of many types of musculoskeletal pain. During a Chiropractic manipulation the joint is placed in a safe position and moved within its natural range of motion. It can be used to treat the spine or extremities. On occasion a "cracking" noise can be heard. If you are a newcomer to manipulation we are happy to walk you through it in detail to make your experience as comfortable as possible.

Kinesiology Taping

Kinesiology tape is a specialized athletic tape that has been developed to help aid the body's natural healing process without inhibiting the body's ability to move. KT is beneficial to the reduction of swelling/bruising, scar correction, pain reduction, joint stabilization and many other uses

Functional Examination/Rehabilitation

While injuries seem like an isolated entity, the reality is the body has to work as a whole to accomplish mechanical tasks. As a result, often times the site of the pain is not always the root of the problem. In many cases the injured structure had been doing the job of others around it that have stopped functioning properly. By taking a patient through a series of everyday movement patterns we can identify structures are not functioning properly. A personalized set of exercises and/or stretches will then be created for each patient's individual needs to not only aid in the recovery process but prevent future re-injury.

Soft Tissue Mobilization

This would include techniques like instrument assisted soft tissue, deep tissue massage and pin and stretch techniques depending on the needs and tolerance of each patient. These techniques aid in breaking up adhesions/scar tissue as well as promote blood flow to the area which helps encourage natural metabolic process like bringing nutrients to and carrying waste products aways from the site of treatment. This helps optimize the body's natural healing process.

Acupuncture

Acupuncture originated in China about 10,000 years ago. Small stainless steel needles are used to restore the proper flow of Qi (pronounced "chee"). Acupuncture points are places on the surface of the body where Qi and blood gather. The needles are very small and as a result do not cause tissue damage. Acupuncture is effective in treating many different ailments from anxiety to sinus headaches to infertility.

Dry Needling

Dry needling is the use of solid filiform needles to treat myofascial pain. Research has shown that placing a needle in the trigger point can provide relief to the area without the injection of a medication or an analgesic.
